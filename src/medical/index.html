Robotics

Robotics has a huge breadth of possibility in the medical field from helping people recover from debilitating diseases to assisting in operations themselves.

  • Surgical robots like the da Vinci enable surgeons to make much more precise movements that enable surgery that is less invasive. This offers recovery times and less mistakes in procedures.
  • Disinfection robots like the Xenex provide hospitals with the ability to fully sterilise operating theaters and wards after use by using pulsed UV rays.
  • Medical Delivery Drones enable the delivery of time-sensitive items like blood or plasma to be delivered to remote areas in a short amount of time, improving health outcomes particularly in rural areas. Companies like Zipline have been delivering blood and plasma to remote areas of the United States, Rwanda, Ghana, Nigeria, the Ivory Coast and Kenya.

Medical Image Computing (MIC)

MIC is one field where computing has made a hugely positive contribution to the world. In the past, medical imaging was very limited in scope due to x-ray technology without computers only capable of 2D images. But through the power of computing, scans can now create 3D representations of the inside of someone's body allowing to carefully examine every miniscule detail and potentially find problems earlier and more accurately.
