Applications of Computing in Scientific Research

Introduction

"The most emerging tool in the research process is the computer" (1). This statement is echoed through the widespread use of computer algorithms in academia. Often, many undergraduate science degrees even require some module in the basics of designing computer algorithms. These applications range from pure number-crunching to complex analysis. For example, a phycisist might need to know how to create a simulation, while a mathematician might need to compute large equations.

Data Processing

Computers can process large amounts of data with incredible speed compared to the average person. This creates improvements in productivity and can open opportunities in analysing data that might have seemed impossible before.

Literature Review

Computers can store records of journals and other scientific documents which can be used for a more optimal search of past literature or, alternatively, compare a finalised document to both detect and prevent plagiarism.
